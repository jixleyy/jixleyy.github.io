French Grammar Focus: Present Tense
Regular -ER Verbs Conjugation

In French, regular -ER verbs follow a predictable pattern in the present tense:

Parler (to speak):
  • Je parle (I speak)
  • Tu parles (You speak - informal)
  • Il/Elle parle (He/She speaks)
Examples in Sentences:

Je parle français. - I speak French.

Tu parles anglais? - Do you speak English?

Elle parle espagnol. - She speaks Spanish.

Kanji of the Day: 学 (Manabu - Study)

学

Manabu

To study, to learn

Strokes: 8

On Yomi: ガク (gaku)

Kun Yomi: まな-ぶ (manabu)

Examples:

  • 学校 (gakkou) - school
  • 学生 (gakusei) - student
  • 勉強 (benkyou) - study

French Verb Conjugation: Être (To Be)

Subject French English

Tu es You are (informal)

Example Sentences:
