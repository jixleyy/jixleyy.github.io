Formal vs. Informal Greetings

French greetings vary depending on the social context. Here are the most common greetings:

Formal Greetings
Bonjour
Hello (formal)
Bonsoir
Good evening
Comment allez-vous?
How are you? (formal)
Informal Greetings
Salut
Hi/Bye (informal)
Ça va?
How are you? (informal)
À plus tard
See you later

Cultural Notes

French greetings often include la bise (kissing on the cheeks) in informal settings. The number of kisses varies by region. In formal settings, a handshake is more appropriate.

Pronunciation Practice

Listen and repeat each phrase. Pay attention to the subtle differences in pronunciation.

Bonjour

[bon-ZHOOR] - Formal greeting meaning "good day"

Ça va?

[sa VA] - Informal way to ask "How are you?"

Bonsoir

[bon-SWAR] - Evening greeting meaning "good evening"

Quick Tips
  • Always use "Bonjour" when entering a shop or restaurant
  • "Salut" can mean both "hi" and "bye" in informal contexts
  • "Bonsoir" is used after 6 PM
  • Don't forget to say "Au revoir" when leaving
